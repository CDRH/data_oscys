Teresa Shorter.
against
Anne Chunn.

Be it remembered, that heretofore towit on the Twelfth day of January in the year of our Lord One thousand seven hundred and ninety two; Teresa Shorter by Gabriel Duvall her attorney, preferred and filed in the General Court here, her Petition for Freedom, for herself and her Five children, which is in form following towit. To the honorable the Judges of the General Court: The Petition of Teresa Shorter, humbly sheweth that she, together with her Five children are held in Slavery by Mrs Anne Chunn of Charles County, altho' she is entitled to her Freedom, being descended in the female line from Elizabeth Shorter a Free white woman. She therefore prays your honors, the premises considered to discharge her from further custody, & in the mean time to grant to your Petitioner an order to prevent her present Mistress from selling her out of the State. And she will pray. G. Duvall. 12th Jan. 1792.

Thereupon, it is ordered by the Court here, that the writ of the State of Maryland of Summons issue against the said Anne Chunn to answer unto the Petition aforesaid of the said Teresa Shorter, which was accordingly issued, directed to the Sheriff of Charles County in form following towit. The State of Maryland towit, To the Sheriff of Charles County, Greeting. You are hereby commanded to summon Anne Chunn late of Charles County, that all delays and excuses be set aside she be and appear before the General Court to be held at the City of Annapolis on the second Tuesday of May next, to answer unto the Petition of Teresa Shorter, preferred against her for Freedom: Hereof she is not to fail, and fail not at your peril, and have you then and there this writ. Witness the honorable Samuel Chase Esquire Chief Judge of the said Court the Twenty ninth day of December in the year of our Lord One thousand seven hundred and ninety one. Issued the 12th day of January 1792. (G. Duvall) John Gwinn Clk.

 

At which said second tuesday of May in the year of our Lord one thousand seven hundred and ninety two, being the day of the return of the said aforegoing writ of Summons, comes into the General Court here the said Teresa Shorter by her attorney aforesaid, And the Sheriff of Charles County to whom the said writ of Summons was inform aforesaid directed, makes return thereof to the Court here, thus endorsed towit, "Non Est. James Simmes, Sheriff." And thereupon the parties aforesaid by their attornies aforesaid agree to submit this case to the decision of the Court here on the Testimony filed in Court here in this Case: Whereupon all and singular the premises between the parties aforesaid by the Court here being seen, heard and fully understood, and mature deliberation thereupon had, for that it appears to the Court here, that the said Teresa Shorter the petitioner aforesaid, is entitled to her Freedom, being descended in the female line from Elizabeth Shorter a Free white woman, as she by her petition to the Court here hath alleged: Therefore it is considered by the Court here, that the said Teresa Shorter the petitioner aforesaid, and her Five children towit, Phillis, John, Moses, Charity and Henny be free and discharged of and from the service of the said Anne Chunn; And that she the said Teresa Shorter the petitioner aforesaid, and her Five children towit, the said Phillis, John, Moses, Charity and Henry go thereof discharged, and soforth.
