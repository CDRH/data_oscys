         <p>Mr. Richard Ratcliffe - Alexandria July 3rd, 1786</p>

         <p>Sir</p>
     
         <p>The negro woman named Terry. which you this day have distrained on for Taxes &amp; Levies due you. <span class="gap illegible">[illegible]</span> the favour of you to sell Immediately. together with her son Benjamin as I would not part them on any acct. I give you full power &amp; Liberty to sell them on Friday or Saturday next at the Courthouse in Alexandria. for what they will fetch in ready money. &amp; will Indemnify you for so doing. you <span class="unclear illegible">[returning?]</span> me the <span class="unclear illegible">[overplush?]</span> to the discharge of a Debt due from me to a certain Samuel Hepburn. for which I now am in Custody. it will answer the same purpose. &amp; if any <span class="unclear illegible">[overplush?]</span> shoud. there remain in your hands. it is to be paid to me on my Order. Which will be fully satisfactory to me. &amp; for so doing this shall be your Indemnification.</p>

         <p>Signd. Thomas Ray</p>

         <p>Rd. Ratcliffe <span class="gap illegible">[illegible]</span>
            <br/>
Fairfax</p>

         <p>The within signed &amp; acknowledged in the presence of us<br/>
Signd. Wm Allison<br/>
John English</p> 

         <p>(Endorsed) - 1786. July 8th. Recd. of Richd. Ratcliffe the full ballance of sale of the written slaves Terry &amp; Benjamin &amp; Clear him from any further Demands agst. him. Witness my hand<br/>
Signd. Ths. Ray</p>

         <p>Test. Daniel Carroll<br/>
his x mark</p>

         <p>Further <span class="unclear illegible">[Backd.?]</span> on sd writing<br/>
Thos. Rays Rect. &amp;c about negro child sold July 8th 1786.</p>
