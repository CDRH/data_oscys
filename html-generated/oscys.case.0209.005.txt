<p>District of Columbia, to wit:<br></br>
The United States of America,<br></br>
To the Marshal of the District of Columbia, Greeting:</p>

</div>

<div class="body ">

<p>You are hereby commanded to summon William Addison if he shall be found within the county of Washington, that (all excuses set aside) he be and appear before the Circuit Court of the District of Columbia, to be held for the County of Washington, in the City of Washington, on the fourth Monday of November next, to <del class="testify on the part of">testify on the part of</del> answer the Petition for freedom of Susan Wilson by <del class="her">her</del> John Moore her next friend filed in the said Court against him.</p>

<p>Hereof fail not at your peril; and have you then and there this precept.</p>

<p>Witness, W Cranch Esquire, Chief Judge of the said Court.</p>

<p>Issued the 23d day of August 1836.</p>

<p>W Brent, Clk</p>

<p>Bradley</p>
