<p>Dear Sir,</p>
                
<p>I did not receive yr letter until it was too late to prepare the enclosed copies&#x97;
I hope, however no inconvenience will result&#x97; You did not write for a copy of
the will; but presuming you might want it, I had it copied. if you should
not&#x97; deduct $1 <sup>40</sup>⁄<sub>100</sub>
from $8 <sup>40</sup>⁄<sub>100</sub> and it will leave $7 due me for the
other copies.</p>
                
<p>By a reference to the real Estate w.ch John Thompson died seized
of, and was assessed to, I find it contained 1276¼ acres which                        

<span class="hr"> </span><span class="pageimage"><a href="http://cdrhsites.unl.edu/figures/oscys/large/oscys.case.0036.003.002.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://cdrhsites.unl.edu/figures/oscys/large/oscys.case.0036.003.002.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;"><img src="http://cdrhsites.unl.edu/figures/oscys/thumbnails/oscys.case.0036.003.002.jpg" class="display"></img></a></span>
                
was valued by the assessor to $2340. <del class="Generally speaking">Generally speaking</del> Lands
generally in this county particularly, are assessed to about one third of their
real value wch would make this land worth $7,020 but in
this case I doubt whether it is worth that sum&#x97;</p>
                
<p>Yrs with great respect<br></br>
Jas Forrest<br></br>
15 June 1816.</p>
                
<p>Fra. Scott Key Esq</p>

<p>In looking over the papers of the late Jas Thompson I find
the opinion of Messrs Martin &amp; <span class="illegible">[Mesin?]</span> on the subject of
Jno Thompsons <del class="Estate">Estate</del> will&#x97; particularly as it relates
to the widows third &amp; <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span>.</p>
                
<p>J F.</p>
