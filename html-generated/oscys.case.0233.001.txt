<p>District of Columbia, to Wit:<br></br>
The United States of America,<br></br>
To the Marshal of the District of Columbia, Greeting:</p>

</div>

<div class="body ">

<p>We command you that you <del class="take">take</del> sum. Nathaniel Suit late of Washington County, if he shall be found within the County of Washington in your said District, and him safely keep, so that you have his body before the Circuit Court of the District of Columbia, to be held for the County aforesaid, at the City of Washington, on the third Monday of October next, to answer <del class="unto">unto</del> the Petition for Freedom of John Edward Butler by William Butler his next friend.</p>

<p>Hereof fail not, at your peril, and have you then and there this writ.</p>

<p>Witness W. Cranch Esquire, Chief Judge of our said Court, at the City of Washington, the 5h day of August Anno Domini one thousand eight hundred and 50.</p>

<p>Issued the 24th day of August 1850.</p>

<p>Jno A. Smith clk</p>

<p>C.S. Wallach</p>
