         <p>On this 21st day of June 1832 personally appears before the subscriber a Justice of
            the Peace, Joseph Booth &amp; makes oath on the holy Evangelly of Almighty God that on
            Tuesday Eventing the 19th 
            <del class="ninth">ninth</del> Inst. 
            <span class="">[]</span> This deponent was at Saml. Websters House referred to in the affdt. of Farrill Riley
            at the time Riley went there. That this Deponent remained their all that night &amp; next
            morning went with said Webster to Work in the Country, 
            <del class="h">h</del> and he has no hesitation in saying that the 
            <del class="conversation">conversation</del> representation of a conversation between said Webster &amp; said Riley as represented
            by said Riley is wholly untrue. That no such conversation did take place, nor nothing
            like it, nor was the subject of the negroes talked of at all. That if it had this
            deponent must have heard it as he was present all the time
         </p>
         
         
         <p>Sworn before Geo Naylor J. P.</p>
         
         
      </div>

         <p>On this 21st day of June 1832 personally Saml. Webster before the subscriber &amp; Justice
            of the Peace in &amp; for the county aforesaid &amp; makes oath on the holy evangelic of Almighty
            God that the statement of Favill Riley made in his affidavit of the 20th Inst. filed
            in case of petition for freedom of Nelly Lemmon &amp; children is false throughout, except
            as to his being 
            <del class="present">present</del> at my house the evening he speaks of &amp; perhaps he may have been present when Nelly
            said she wanted me as a witness that I have no recollection about, but the conversation
            he has set forth as having passed between Nelly &amp; me &amp; between him and me are totally
            false, nothing of the kind ever took place, nor did any thing like it
         </p>
         
         
         <p>Sworn to before me
            <br></br>
            George Naylor J. Peace
         </p>
