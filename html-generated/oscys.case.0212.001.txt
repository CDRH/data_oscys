         <p>To the Honble The Judges of the Circuit Court of the District of Columbia for the
            County of Washington
         </p>
         
         
      </div>
      
      
      <div class="body ">
         
         
         <p>The several petitions of Thomas Butler &amp; Sarah his wife, Matilda; Ainey, Reason, &amp;
            Sally children of said Thomas &amp; Sarah and Siddy &amp; Eliza grand children of said Thomas
            &amp; Sarah the two last by said Thomas their grand father and next friend respectfully
            and humbly sets forth to your honors that they are persons of colour who are entitled
            to their freedom and who are now held in a state of slavery by one Gabriel Duval contrary
            to law &amp; to your petitioners just &amp; legal rights and that they are about to be sold
            into slavery out of this district to some foreign purchasers wherefor they severally
            pray your Honors to grant them such 
            <del class="to">to</del> relief as they may be lawfully entitled to and such process of your honorable court
            against the said Gabriel Duvall as is usual in such cases to compel the attendance
            of the said Gabriel in your honorable court to answer this petition and to enter the
            usual security and recognizance not to remove your petitioners or any of them out
            of the jurisdiction of your Honorable Court &amp; they as in duty bound will ever pray
            &amp;c
         </p>
         
         
         <p>Robert Beale</p>
         
         
         <p>District of Columbia
            <br></br>
            Washington County ss
         </p>
         
         
         <p>Appears before me a Justice of the Peace in &amp; for this County. The Person above named
            &amp; 
            <span class="">[]</span> in this Petition and made oath in due form of Law to the facts therein stated.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>Given under my hand &amp; Seal this 10 June 1828</p>
         
         
         <p>Jno Chalmers (seal)</p>
