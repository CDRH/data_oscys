      <div class="caption"> 

         <p>Negro Robert Thomas v. Elizabeth Magruder.</p>

      </div>

      <div class="syllabus"> 

         <p>A record copy of a deed of emancipation may be given in evidence by the petitioner, upon trial of a petition for freedom, without producing the original or accounting for its non-production.</p>

      </div>

      <div class="body">

         <p>Petition for freedom.</p>

         <p>On the trial, Mr. Key, for the petitioner, offered in evidence the record of the deed of manumission from Reginald Magruder, recorded in the office of the clerk of this Court.</p>

         <p>Mr. Z. C. Lee, objected and called for the original, and proof by the subscribing witnesses.</p>

         <p>The Court (Morsell, J., absent,) refused to require Mr. Key to produce the original, and permitted him to use the copy on the record; it having been acknowledged and recorded according to the <span class="bibl code">Act of Maryland, 1796, c. 67, §§ 28, 29.</span>
         </p>
