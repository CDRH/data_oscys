         <p>To the Honorable the Judges of the circuit court of the County of Washington in the
            District of Columbia—
         </p>
         
         
      </div>
      
      
      <div class="body ">
         
         
         <p>The humble Petition of Davy Davis, humbly sheweth, that your
            Petitioner is detained in slavery by James Cassin —
            and as he is born free he prays that process may be awarded to compell the
            appearance of the said James Cassin to answer his
            complaint.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>Edm J Lee for the Complaintant—
            <br></br>
            17th June 1809
         </p>
         
         
         
         
         <p>Your petitioner begs leave to state that the said James
            Cassin is about to remove him without the District of
            Columbia &amp; to take him to New Orleans,
            for the purpose of there selling of him, that he has already confined your
            petitioner in irons for that purpose, your petitioner therefore prays that
            process may
            immediately be issued to bring the said
            
            
            <span class="hr"> </span>
            <span class="pageimage">
               <a href="http://earlywashingtondc.org/files/figures/large/p002.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://earlywashingtondc.org/files/figures/large/p002.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;">
                  <img src="http://earlywashingtondc.org/files/figures/thumbnails/p002.jpg" class="display"></img>
               </a>
            </span>
            
            
            <span class="persName">James Cassin</span> before the court for the purpose of compelling
            him not to remove your petitioner out of the district &amp; to permit him to
            appear &amp; assert in court his right to his freedom as in Duty bound he will
            ever pray—
         </p>
         
         
         
         
         <p>194</p>
         
         
         <p>Davy Davis
            <br></br>
            vs.
            <br></br>
            Jas. Cassin
         </p>
