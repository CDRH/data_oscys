      <div class="body ">
         
         
         <p>Mem. on the trial of the cause the 
            <del class="Dft">Dft</del> Petn offer'd in Evidence the depn of Mrs Rankin in these 
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span>) 
            <del class="whereupon">whereupon</del> and thereupon pray'd the Ct to instruct the Jury that if &amp;c (here insert Mr Key's
            prayer) which instruction the Court refused to give but 
            <del class="whereupon the Ct">whereupon the Ct</del> instructed the Jury that
         </p>
         
         
         <p>If they 
            <del class="Jury">Jury</del> should be of opinion from the Evidence that Mrs Rankin, whose deposition has been
            read in Evidence as aforesaid was, at the time she brought the petitioner into the
            city of Washington as aforesaid, a citizen of the U.S. 
            <del class="a she one of State of Virginia United States">a she one of State of Virginia United States</del> coming  
            <del class="to reside">to reside</del> into the city of Washington aforesaid, with a bona fide intention of settling 
            <del class="to reside">to reside</del> therein, then her said importation of the said Slave was lawful and did not 
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span> the Petn to her freedom whether the said importation were or were not made with the
            consent of the Dft. But that if the Jury shd be of opinion that 
            <del class="she">she</del> sd Mrs Rankin was 
            <del class="not">not</del> not a citizen of the 
            <del class="U. S.">U. S.</del> 
            <del class="District of Columbia">District of Columbia</del> U.S. coming into the sd city of W. 
            <del class="to">to</del> with the intention of settling therein, then such importation of the said slave was
            unlawful and entitles the Petitioner to her freedom; whether such importation were
            or were not with the consent of the Dft.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>To the first 
            <del class="part">part</del> branch of which instruction made by Petrs counsel 
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span> the refusal of the Prayer the Petitioner excepts, and to the second branch thereof
            the Dft excepts, and this their part and several bill of exceptions is signed sealed
            and enrolled.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>W. Cranch (seal)
            <br></br>
            B. Thruston (seal)
         </p>

         <p>If the Jury believes from the evidence that the Deft knew of the intended importation
            of the Petitioner by Mr Rankin &amp; did not object to it then such importation by Mr
            Rankin entitles the petitioner to her freedom. Further it is competent to the Jury
            to infer from 
            <del class="the">the</del> his knowing of the importation &amp; not objecting to it that such imporatation was made
