         <p>To the Honble the Judges of the circuit court of the United States for the district
            of Columbia &amp; county of Washington
         </p>
         
         
      </div>
      
      
      <div class="body ">
         
         
         <p>The petition of Susan Bordley &amp; her children Rachael Bordley, 
            Charlotte Bordley, Phillis Bordley &amp; James Bordley 
            humbly sheweth, That your petitioner first named Susan Bordley is a native of the
            state of 
            Pennsylvania &amp; was entitled by the laws of said state 
            to her freedom but to prevent her enjoying the benefit of the laws of the said State,
            she was forcibly brought 
            from said state by her then master &amp; sold on certain conditions in the state of Maryland.
            
            That since her arrival here she became the mother of the other persons above named
            on whose behalf she now 
            petitions, &amp; that she together with her children are now unlawfully claimed &amp; detained
            
            in servitude by a certain Anne Tilley Your petitioner therefore prays on behalf of
            herself 
            &amp; her children, that your Honors would take her case &amp; her rights into consideration,
            &amp; 
            authorize &amp; direct such steps on the
            
            
            <span class="hr"> </span>
            <span class="pageimage">
               <a href="http://earlywashingtondc.org/files/figures/large/p002.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://earlywashingtondc.org/files/figures/large/p002.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;">
                  <img src="http://earlywashingtondc.org/files/figures/thumbnails/p002.jpg" class="display"></img>
               </a>
            </span>
            
            premises as to the honble Court may appear meet. And your petitioner &amp; her children
            will forever pray &amp;c.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>John Law Petr's atty.</p>
