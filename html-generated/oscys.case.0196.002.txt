<p>District of Columbia, to wit:<br/> 
The United States of America,<br/>
To the Marshal of the District of Columbia, Greeting:</p>

</div>

<div class="body ">

<p>We command you that you <del>take</del> summon Hope H. Slatter late of Washington County, if he shall be found within the County of Washington in your said District, <del>and safely keep, so that you have</del> that <del>bod</del> he be and appear before the Circuit Court of the District of Columbia, to be held for the County aforesaid, at the City of Washington, on the fourth Monday of March next, to answer <del>unto</del> the Petition for freedom of Robert Trunnel filed in said Court against him.</p>

<p>Hereof fail not, at your peril, and have you then and there this writ.</p>

<p>Witness W Cranch Esquire, Chief Judge of our said Court, at the City of Washington, the 31st day of Dec Anno Domini one thousand eight hundred and forty one.</p>

<p>Issued the 3rd day of Jany 1842.</p>

<p>W. Brent Clk</p>

<p>Hoban</p>
