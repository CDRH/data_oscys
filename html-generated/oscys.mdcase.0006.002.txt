      <div class="body">

         <p>Petitioners Interrogatories</p>
     
         <p>1. Do you know the parties in this suit, and how long have you known them?</p>

         <p>2. Do you know the mother of the petitioner, and how long have you known her?</p>

         <p>3. Do you know the grandmother of the petitioner, &amp; how long did you know her?</p>

         <p>4. Did you know Betty Mingoe a mulatto woman who formerly lived with Mrs. Pile, mother of the defendant, if yea, when &amp; how long did you know her, and do you know or have you heard, and from whom, and when, that Betty Mingoe was <del>a</del> descended from a white woman or a freewoman? Relate your knowledge fully and freely.</p>

         <p>G. Duvall for Pet.</p>

      <div class="caption">

         <p>Charles County Set</p>

      <div class="body">

         <p>On this day to wit the 13th day of September 1794 Walter Hanson came before me and was Examined on his oath touching the petition of Robert Thomas against Henry Pile in the General Court of Maryland as to the Interogatories on behalf of the Plaintiff to depose and Say to the first that he knows of no person by the name of Robert Thomas as to Henry Pile: if he be the Gentleman that is a Catholic Priest I have seen him several times but no particular acquaintance with him</p>

         <p>As to the 2nd Interogatory he knows nothing of the mother of the Petitioner</p>

         <p>In Answer the Third he knows nothing of the <del>mot</del> grand mother of the Petitioner.</p>

         <p>In answer to the fourth the Deponent deposes to know nothing at all of Betty Mingo - nor did he ever to his knowledge hear any thing of her until the petition for freedom was preferrd and further this deponent Saith not</p>

         <p>In answer to the first Interogatory taken for the Defendant Henry Pile The Deponent Walter Hanson on his oath declares that he was very well acquainted with Thomas Stone Father of David Stone, with David Stone and Catherine Stone relict of Thomas Stone <span class="unclear illegible">[Moreso?]</span> that <span class="unclear illegible">[Seventy?]</span> years ago that he has lived within four miles of Pointon Manor since the year Seventeen hundred and thirty Eight</p>

         <p>In answer to the Second Interogatory the deponent Says that David Stone did marry his Sister he Remembers they lived in a Married State in the year 1745 when she died but before her death she had three Children that he was well acquainted with David Stone before that marriage took place</p>

         <p>To the third Interogatory he Says Samuel Stone did marry his Daughter.</p>

         <p>To the fourth he deposes he has been well acquainted with the Stone family ever Since he was fifteen or Sixteen years old he this deponent being now in his Eighty third year</p>

         <p>To the fifth he says that he does not Know David Stone gave to his Son Samuel any negroes other than those who he has been told has Set up a Claim to Freedom he Knows not which of them now Claim a Title to Freedom only from Hearsay</p>

         <p>To the Sixth he deposes he know nothing of Betty Mingo or any

<span class="hr"> </span>
            <span class="pageimage">
               <a href="http://cdrhsites.unl.edu/figures/oscys/large/oscys.mdcase.0006.002.007.jpg"
                  rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]"
                  title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://cdrhsites.unl.edu/figures/oscys/large/oscys.mdcase.0006.002.007.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;">
                  <img src="http://cdrhsites.unl.edu/figures/oscys/thumbnails/oscys.mdcase.0006.002.007.jpg"
                       class="display &#xA;            "/>
               </a>
            </span>

any of her Descendants nor whether they were held as Slaves by any person whatever nor does he know why the Negroes in Question went by the Name of Mingo or from what Source they took the name of Thomas</p>

         <p>To the Eighth Interogatory he says that the Character of Margaret Reeder Sarah Fowke Mary Stone Mary McCoy Anne Thompson Richard Robin Reeder in his oppinion Stands as fair and unblemished as any persons he Ever was acquainted with.</p>

         <p>To the Ninth he says that Charles Ray and Thomas Webster are poor free mulattos but reckond very Honest and Inoffensive persons</p>

         <p>To the tenth he says he thinks he has heard of Mary Curtis but knows nothing of her Character</p>

         <p>To the Eleventh he says he apprehends that Mr. Richard Barnes William Maconchie and James Mancaster are likely to know the Events that happened in the Stone Family as they are of Long standing and near neighbours and acquaintance And further this deponent Saith not</p>

         <p>Walter Hanson</p>

         <p>I Certifie that the above deposition of Walter Hanson was taken by me the day and years first above written</p>

         <p>James Freeman.</p>
