         <p>To the Honourable the Judges of the Circuit Court of the District of Columbia for
            the County of Washington the Petition of Morris otherwise called Peter Morris otherwise
            called Peer Morris humbly sheweth that the said Morris is a free person of colour
            formerly resident of the state of New York and 
            <del class="was">was</del> is now unlawfully held and detained in close confinement &amp; imprisonment by one George
            Miller of the city of Washington as the property &amp; slave of certain persons whose
            names are not known &amp; to whom the said Morris was unlawfully sold by one Haley. He
            therefore prays that the United States writ of Habeas Corpus may be issued to the
            said George Miller directed commanding him forthwith to have the body of your petitioner
            before your honors or one of you together with the cause of his imprisonment &amp; detention
            to do &amp; receive &amp;c. in due form 
            <del class="of law">of law</del> according to Law &amp; to the statute in such case made &amp; provided that he may be discharged
            from his unlawful imprisonment, and that such other relief may be given to the said
            Morris in the premises at the nature of his case may require &amp;c. And also that a subpoena
            may be issued to the said George Miller directed commanding him to appear &amp; answer
            to yr. Petitioners claim of freedom &amp; that such other relief may be extended as the
            nature of the case requires &amp;..
         </p>
         
         
         <p>
            <del class="(J. Wiley for Petr.)">(J. Wiley for Petr.)</del>
         </p>
         
         
         <p>J. Wiley for Petr.</p>

         <p>On this fifteenth day of May A.D. 1816 before the subscriber an United States Justice
            of the peace in &amp; for Washington County personally appeared William A. Scott 
            <del class="and made">and made</del> one of the people commonly called Quakers or Friends and made his solemn affirmation
            that he hath been informed by several persons &amp; believes that the coloured person
            called Morris named in the within petition 
            <del class="is now">is now</del> was brought to Washington City by one 
            <del class="Daley">Daley</del> Haley who said that he had bought the said Morris but that Morris thought that he
            was only hired to him &amp; he did not wish Morris to know it: that said Morris claimed
            to be free &amp; said he had hired himself to said Haley; that said Haley afterwards sold
            said Morris clandestinely &amp; immediately went away from Washington: that the said Morris
            was thereupon committed to the custody of 
            <del class="said">said</del> George Miller by whom he is now kept in close confinement in his house, notwithstanding
            his claim of freedom.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>Jno. Peter</p>
