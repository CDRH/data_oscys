         <p>And the said Petitioner by Key &amp; Dunlop by attornies saith that the Deft. George Milburn,
            by anything in his said plea above alledged, ought not to be dismissed without further
            plea or answer to the said Petitioner's petition, as prayed in the said plea, because
            (protesting that he is not the slave of the said Milburn nor of any other person or
            persons) he saith that the said Deft Milburn had at the time of his said petition
            and claimed an interest in the petitioner as his slave, having purchased him of a
            certain Louisa J. Southall by an absolute unconditional contract and 
            <del class="after">after</del> this he 
            <del class="is ready to">is ready to</del>
