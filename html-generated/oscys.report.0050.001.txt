      <div class="syllabus"> 

         <p>An absolute deed of goods and chattels need not be recorded; and a record copy is not evidence.</p>

      </div>

      <div class="body">

         <p>Action for Freedom.</p>

         <p>Mr. Key and Mr. Hodgson, for the plaintiff, offered in evidence the record of a deed of personal property.</p>

         <p>Mr. Taylor, for the defendant, objected that a record copy of an absolute deed of good and chattels, for valuable consideration, need not be recorded, and derives no validity therefrom; and a record copy is not evidence.</p>

         <p>And such was the opinion of the Court (<em class="term">nem. con.</em>)</p>

         <p>(See <span class="bibl treatise">statue of Frauds of Virginia, p. 16</span>.)</p>
