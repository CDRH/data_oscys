      <div class="body">

         <p>Matilda, a woman of colour, called and known as Matilda Sewall, complains of Eleanor Thompson in custody &amp;c. of a plea.</p>

         <p>for that the said Eleanor Thompson on the first day of January in the year Eighteen hundred &amp; thirty three with force and arms, to wit: with swords, staves, sticks and fists, made an assault on the said Plaintiff, to wit, at the county aforesaid, and then &amp; there beat, bruised, wounded and ill-treated her, and then &amp; there imprisoned her, &amp; kept &amp; detained her iin prison for a long time, to wit, for the space of Four months then next following, until she was forced &amp; obliged to lay out &amp; expend, &amp; did lay out &amp; expend a large sum of money, to wit: the sum of Fifty Dollars in the obtaining of her discharge from such her imprisonment, without any reasonable or probable cause whatsoever, contrary to the laws &amp; customs of the United States, and against the will the said Matilda Sewall; and then &amp; there did other wrongs to the said Matilda against the peace &amp; dignity of the United States, and to the Damage of the said Matilda One Thousand Dollars, and therefore she brings her suit &amp;c.</p>

         <p>B. Hooe P. Q.</p>
