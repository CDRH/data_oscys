      <div class="body">
     
         <p>William McConchie being duly sworn before James Freeman one of the Justices of Charles County on the interrogatories exhibited to him on the part of the Petitioner answereth as follows: viz</p>

         <p>1st No neither.</p>

         <p>2 I do not know her;</p>

         <p>3 I know nothing of her.</p>

         <p>4 I know nothing of her &amp; am ignorant totally of her descent.</p>
     
         <p>Answers to Defts Interrogatories</p>

         <p>1 I knew Peg a black woman belonging to David Stone held as a slave; She was always treated as a slave; and labored as a slave. She was sold or exchanged to John Semple whom she served as a slave.</p>

         <p>2 I have lived great part of my time on Point and Manor, and now within a mile and a half and have never lived farther from it.</p>

         <p>3 I was not acquainted with Thomas Stone father of David Stone, nor Catharine Stone

            <del>Nor Cat</del> widow of Thomas Stone. I was well acquainted with David Stone, almost all my life. My eldest Brother married a daughter of David Stone. I was in general on friendly terms with him and his family.</p>

         <p>I never heard of their having held persons, <del>in slave</del> entitled to their freedom in slavery.</p>

         <p>4 Not that I recollectt.</p>

         <p>William McConchie sworn before the Subscriber aged about fifty seven or fifty eight years this 11th day October 1794</p>

         <p>James Freeman</p>

         <p>Wm. McConchie</p>
