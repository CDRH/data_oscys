<p>Priscilla Queen<br></br>
v.<br></br>
Francis Neale</p>

</div>

<div class="body ">
                 
<p>On the trial of this cause the Petitioner offered to examine as a witness
                    Simon Queen, a free negro <del class="and">and</del> not in a state of servitude by law, and produced in evidence
                    a record &amp; certificate of his freedom in the words &amp; figures following,
                    to wit, (here insert them.)</p>
                    
<p>and it is admitted that the <span class="illegible">[authentication?]</span> of the record is <span class="illegible">[regular?]</span> and
                    that he is the same <span class="persName">Simon Queen</span>
                    therein <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span> and it is also
                    admitted that the Deft is a free Christian white person and a regularly ordained
                    minister of the Roman Catholic Church. and  the Deft's counsel objected to the
                    admission of the said Simon as a witness; but the Court overruled the objection
                    &amp; admitted him to be sworn &amp; examined as a witness. To which the Deft's
                    counsel excepts &amp; this his bill of exceptions is sealed &amp; enrolled.</p>
                
<p>(June 21. 1810)</p>
                
<p>W. Cranch (seal)<br></br>
N. Fitzhugh (seal)</p>
