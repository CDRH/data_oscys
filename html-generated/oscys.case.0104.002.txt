<p>District of Columbia, to wit:</p>

<p>The United States of America,</p>

<p>To the Marshal of the District of Columbia, Greeting:</p>

</div>

<div class="body ">

<p>You are hereby commanded to summon Gerard T. Greenfield if he shall be found in the County of Washington, that (all excuses set aside) he be and appear before the Circuit Court of the District of Columbia, to be held for the County of Washington, in the City of Washington, on fourth Monday of March next, to <del>testify on the part of</del> the petition for freedom of Ann Bell &amp; children Daniel &amp; David filed in the said Court against him and so forth</p>

<p>Hereof, fail not at your peril; and have you then and there this precept.</p>

<p>Witness, W Cranch Esquire, Chief Judge of the said Court.</p>

<p>Issued the 24 day of December 1836.</p>

<p>W Brent Clk</p>

<p>(G. L. Giberson)</p>
