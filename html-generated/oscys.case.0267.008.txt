<p>The deposition of Catherine Appleby, of full age, taken by consent, of the parties Attorneys at Law, in a suit for freedom now depending, in the Circuit Court of the United States, for Washington County in the District of Columbia, in which suit, Jesse Nelson, a Man of color, is the Petitioner, and John Cornwell is deft: which deposition, when taken, is to be read, as evidence, on behalf of the deft: Cornwell, upon the trial of the above cause.</p>

<p>The deponent, being first duly qualified, cautioned, and sworn, to speak the truth, the whole truth, and nothing but the truth, deposeth, and answers, as follows.</p>

<p>Question 1st by Deft's: Counsel.</p>

<p>Are you personally acquainted, with the parties to this suit? Answer - I am</p>

<p>Question 2nd Are your acquainted with one John C. Weedon, &amp; and if yea, state where he resides.</p>

<p>Answer - I am, he resides in Prince William County Va</p>

<p>Question 3. Were you or were you not ever acquainted with a Man named Thomas Nelson, and if yea, is he now dead or living?</p>

<p>Answer I was well acquainted with him &amp; he is now dead</p>

<p>Question the 4th Do you know, or do you not know, who is his Executor, or administrator, if any his estate has?</p>

<p>Answer - John C. Wedon is his administrator</p>

<p>Question 5. You say, that you know Jesse Nelson, the

Petitioner for freedom, in this cause. if so, please state all you know about him, where he was born, where raised, and if no raised in Washington City, in the District of Columbia, how &amp; by what means did he get there. State, particularly, all you know about it/</p>

<p>Answer - I was well acquainted with Thos Nelson as stated in No 3 answer. He is now dead &amp; and has been dead for upwards of seven years. Nelson was the executor of my mother Constance Cornwell, &amp; after he died, John C. Weedon was appointed by the Court of Prince William, his administrator &amp; as such he took into his possession, all the slaves devised to John Cornwell the defendant in this cause &amp; among the number was Jesse the petitioner, &amp; and his brother Albert, which two slaves were both <del class="from">from</del> born &amp; raised in Prince William County Va. John C Weedon when interogated by witness by what authority he sold Jesse &amp; Albert he replied that he had sold them because he wanted money, &amp; when reminded by witness that he had sold property belonging To John Cornwell, &amp; and not to him or Nelson, he said if it was the property of John Cornwell he would have to show it. This conversation took place on a Saturday, &amp; the Sale was made the day before. On Sunday following witness followed after slaves aforesaid, called on her way up to Washington at the establis

hment kept by Mr Bruin near Alexandria &amp; was told that they were not there but that they were taken to Williams Jail in Washington City &amp; if she would go on there she would find them to which place witness went but failed to see them upon her first visit but upon a second visit, on the next day saw them both in Williams Jail spoke to them &amp; shook their hands.</p>

<p>Question 6th Did you, or did you not, promptly inform<del class="ed">ed</del> Cornwell, that his slaves were sold, and if yea, what did Cornwell do, in the matter?</p>

<p>Answer - I did &amp; Cornwell immediately proceeded to take them our of Williams Jail by legal process</p>

<p>Question 7th Is, or is not, John Cornwell, the deft: in this cause, related to Jesse &amp; Albert, &amp; if he is, what is the nature thereof</p>

<p>Answer - he is in no manner related to them, or either of them.</p>

<p>Question 8th Are you positive, that the two men, who you say, you saw in William's Jail, and with whom you shook hands, were Jesse &amp; Albert, and that the Petitioner Jesse is the identical Jesse, that you say, was in William's Jail?</p>

<p>Answer - yes, &amp; Jesse told witness that he had sued John</p>

<p>And further deponent saith not</p>

<p>The undersigned, a Justice of the Peace, in, and for, the County aforesaid, do hereby certify, that the within deposition, of Catharine Appleby, was taken at the <del class="time, and place, in the caption and time">time, and place, in the caption and time</del> Law Office of Chr: Neale, in the City of Alexandria, Virginia, on Saturday the 18th March '54, by consent of parties; that the same, was kept open during that day. that no one, appeared on behalf of the Petitioner; that the undersigned, wrote down the witnesses answers, to the several interrogatories, and after they were all reduced to writing as aforesaid, the same way carefully read over, to the witness, who made her cross mark, to her name, and made oath, in due form of Law, that her testimony contained the truth, the whole truth, and nothing but the truth. and which, is accordingly certified, by the undersigned, a Justice of the Peace, of Alexandria County Virginia, under his hand, and seal, this the 18th March 1854.</p>

<p>Robt Hunter {JP} {Seal}</p>

<p>Fee for taking the above deposition $3.<br></br>
paid by C. Neale.<br></br>
R. Hunter</p>
