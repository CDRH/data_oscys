         <p>Negro Louisa Johnson v. Milo Mason.</p>

      <div class="syllabus ">
         
         
         <p>If a slave be not brought into the county of Washington for sale, nor to reside permanently,
            he is not entitled to freedom under the 
            <div class="bibl code">Maryland Act of 1796, c. 67 </div>.
         </p>
         
         
      </div>
      
      
      <div class="body ">
         
         
         <p>Petition for freedom. The Court (nem. con.) was of opinion
            that if the petitioner was not brought into this county for sale or
            to reside, she is not entitled to freedom under the 
            <div class="bibl code">Maryland Act
               of 1796, c. 67 
            </div>, and that the act meant a permanent residence, a
            residence without expectation of change.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>See the case of 
            <div class="bibl case">Jordan v. Sawyer, in this Court at April term, 1823, [2 Cranch, 373.] </div>
         </p>
