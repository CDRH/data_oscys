<p>Albemarle County, Virginia. any 12, 1820 </p>

<p>I do hereby constitute and appoint Edward Coles Esqr my 
Attorney with full power to execute and sign in my name and on 
my behalf a deed of Manumission, to a negro man, named Lynch  
lately in my possession, so as to secure to him, free from the claim 
of myself, my heirs or assigns, forever, the uninterupted enjoyment of 
his freedom— And I hereby bind myself my heirs &amp;c to 
abide by, and conform to any legal act which my said Attorney may do touching the
premises— Witness my hand and Seal the day and year above written—</p>

<p>J.P. Cocke (Seal)</p>

<p>Teste—<br/>
Chr Cocke<br/>
S. W. Cocke</p>

<span class="hr"> </span><span class="pageimage"><a href="http://cdrhsites.unl.edu/figures/oscys/large/oscys.case.0041.003.002.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://cdrhsites.unl.edu/figures/oscys/large/oscys.case.0041.003.002.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;"><img src="http://cdrhsites.unl.edu/figures/oscys/thumbnails/oscys.case.0041.003.002.jpg" class="display"/></a></span>

<p>I do hereby certify that the within named Lynch having given satisfaction as to 
the claims of James P. Cocke Esq. The latter is content that 
Lynch shall go free: and Mr. Jones is 
authorized &amp; requested to dispose of the suit now pending so as to confirm Lynch's 
title to freedom.</p>

<p>Edward Coles<br/>
Washington Jany 28 1820</p>
