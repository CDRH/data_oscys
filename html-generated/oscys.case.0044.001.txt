<p>To the Honourable the Judges of the Circuit Court for the 
County of Washington, District of Columbia</p>

</div>

<div class="body ">

<p>The petition of Richard Johnson, a negro man of the County of Washington, 
District of Columbia aforesaid, humbly sheweth unto your Honours that sometime about
the year 1782 or 3 your petitioner was held and owned by a certain Thomas Macubbin of 
Montgomery County, State of Maryland. that while so held &amp; 
owned by the said Thomas Macubbin he the said Thomas removed to 
the State of Virginia and removed and imported into the said State of Virginia 
your petitioner to reside. That upon such importation, the Acts of Assembly of the State of Virginia 
required a certain Affidavit to be made by the said Thomas and in the absence of such Affidavit gave 
freedom to your petitioner. That the said Thomas neglected &amp; omitted to make such 
Affidavit whereby your petitioner acquired title to

<span class="hr"> </span><span class="pageimage"><a href="http://cdrhsites.unl.edu/figures/oscys/large/oscys.case.0044.001.002.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://cdrhsites.unl.edu/figures/oscys/large/oscys.case.0044.001.002.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;"><img src="http://cdrhsites.unl.edu/figures/oscys/thumbnails/oscys.case.0044.001.002.jpg" class="display"/></a></span>

freedom.</p>

<p>That after the said Thomas had resided, &amp; held said <del>neg</del> 
petitioner, in the State of Virginia eight or nine years, he removed to the State of Maryland and 
removed and imported your petitioner into the State of Maryland to reside and neglected and omitted to comply with 
certain requisites contained on certain Acts of Assembly of the State of Maryland prohibiting the importation 
of slaves into the State of Maryland then in force. That if your petitioner had not acquired a title to freedom under the 
laws of the State of Virginia by his removal to the said State, then he would have acquired title to freedom by his 
importation to reside into the State of Maryland.</p>

<p>That your petitioner came to the possession of a certain Janet Lingan who now sets up claim 
to him under some pretended or alledged purchase, or bequests or descent. and that your petitioner is now unjustly 
and illegally held in bondage
  
<span class="hr"> </span><span class="pageimage"><a href="http://cdrhsites.unl.edu/figures/oscys/large/oscys.case.0044.001.003.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://cdrhsites.unl.edu/figures/oscys/large/oscys.case.0044.001.003.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;"><img src="http://cdrhsites.unl.edu/figures/oscys/thumbnails/oscys.case.0044.001.003.jpg" class="display"/></a></span>

by the said Janet Lingan.</p>

<p>Your petitioner prays your Honours to take his case into consideration and to 
grant him a fair &amp; impartial trial according to the law of the land and 
your petitioner will ever pray and so forth.</p>

<p>July 3. 1821.</p>

<p>Augt Taney for Petitioner.</p>
