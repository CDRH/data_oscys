<p>District of Columbia, to wit:<br/>
The United States of America,<br/>
To the Marshal of the District of Columbia, Greeting:</p>

</div>

<div class="body ">

<p>We command you that you <del>take</del> summon Sarah E. Linton late of Washington County, if she shall be found within the County of Washington in your said District, <del>and safely keep,</del> so that <del>you</del> she be <del>have</del> and appear <del>bod</del> before the Circuit Court of the District of Columbia, to be held for the County aforesaid, at the City of Washington, on the third Monday of October next, to answer <del>unto</del> the Petition of Sally Cross for her freedom.</p>

<p>Hereof fail not, at your peril, and have you then and there this writ.</p>

<p>Witness Wm Cranch Esquire, Chief Judge of our said Court, at the City of Washington, the 16th day of July Anno Domini one thousand eight hundred and forty five.</p>

<p>Issued the 19th day of July 1845.</p>

<p>Wm Brent Clk</p>

<p>C. Wallh.</p>
