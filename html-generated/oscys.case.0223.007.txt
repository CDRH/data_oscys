         <p>The deposition of 
            <del class="Abrah">Abrah</del> Abraham Jump witness of lawful aft on the part of the defendant
         </p>
         
         
         <p>To the 1st interogatory, this deponent answereth and saith that he is acquainted with
            the parties to the above suit that he has known them about twenty years
         </p>
         
         
         <p>To the 2nd interogatory, this deponent answereth and saith that the above named Petitioner
            is a slave or at lease was a slave to the above named defendant from the time of his
            first acquaintance with him untill he was taken from Maryland sometime in the Spring
            of 1834 by Isaac P Pendeleton during about eight years of 
            <del class="my">my</del> his acquaintance with the above named Petitioner prior to his trippe to Virginia
            he was the house servant of the above named defendant Clarissa Purnell.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>To the 3rd Interogatory, this deponent answereth and saith that some time in the spring
            of 1834 I P Pendleton took with him to Virginia the above named Petitioner and the
            circumstances conected with his removal from Maryland
            
            
            <span class="hr"> </span>
            <span class="pageimage">
               <a href="http://jetson.unl.edu/oscys_figures/large/oscys.case.0223.007.002.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://jetson.unl.edu/oscys_figures/large/oscys.case.0223.007.002.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;">
                  <img src="http://jetson.unl.edu/oscys_figures/thumbnails/oscys.case.0223.007.002.jpg" class="display"></img>
               </a>
            </span>
            
            to Virginia were that a few days prior to his leaving M.D the afsd I P Pendleton then
            an 
            <span class="illegible">[inmate?]</span> of the defendant Clarissa Purnell family was unfortunately engaged in a fracas with
            a 
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span> of M.D to which the add Petitioner was considered 
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span> and the said I P Pendleton not being disposed to submit to the Civil authority and
            throw himself on the mercy of the law 
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span> departed from Maryland to Virginia accompanied by the above named Petitioner. And
            further this deponent saith not.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>To the 4th Interogatory, this deponent canot answer Cross Interogatories to be propounded
            on the part of the petitioner to all the defendants witnesses
         </p>
         
         
         <p>1st Interogatory 
            <del class="what">what</del> this deponent answereth and saith that Miss Clarissa Purnell is about forty years
            of age and in case of her decase, her two sisters viz. Adaline Terrill &amp; Mary M Thondike
            &amp; the children of her late Brother Fredk Turnell &amp; her late sister Serena Pendleton
            would be her heirs
         </p>
         
         
         <p>To the 2nd Interogatory, this deponent answereth and saith that I Purnell Pendleton
            is a relation of Miss Purnell but does not know that he is a favoured relative.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>To the 3rd Interogatory, this deponent answereth &amp; saith that he refers to his answer
            to the 3rd Interagatory on the part  
            
            
            <span class="hr"> </span>
            <span class="pageimage">
               <a href="http://jetson.unl.edu/oscys_figures/large/oscys.case.0223.007.003.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://jetson.unl.edu/oscys_figures/large/oscys.case.0223.007.003.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;">
                  <img src="http://jetson.unl.edu/oscys_figures/thumbnails/oscys.case.0223.007.003.jpg" class="display"></img>
               </a>
            </span>
            
            of the defendant so far as relates to the cause &amp; reason of J P Pendleton to taking
            with him to Virginia the Petitioner. And to the latter part of this Interogatory this
            deponent canot answer.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>To the 4th Interogatory, this deponent answereth &amp; saith that I P Pendleton has resided
            partly in Maryland and partly in Virginia for the last seven years.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>To the 5th Interogatory, this deponent answereth and saith that he never did hear
            either Miss Purnell or I P Pendleton declare the Petitioner to be the property of
            the latter, but 
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span> frequently heard Miss Purnell 
            <del class="say">say</del> speak of the Petitioner as her property, and on all ocasions from the time of his
            leaving Maryland to the present time &amp; those occasions have frequently happened as
            
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span> been transacting 
            <del class="her">her</del> Miss Purnells business on the 
            <span class="illegible">[E L M D?]</span> the whole time of the sd Petioners absence
         </p>
         
         
         <p>To the 6th Interogatory, this deponent answereth and saith that he has never heard
            J P Pendleton at any time 
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span> or represent the Petitioner to be his Property &amp; further this deponent saith not
         </p>
         
         
         <p>To the 7th Interogatory, this deponent canot answer</p>
         
         
         <p>To the 8th Interogatory, this deponent canot answer</p>

         <p>To the 9th Interogatory, this deponent can say nothing</p>
         
         
         <p>Additional Cross Interogatory on the part plff</p>
         
         
         <p>To the 1st Interogatory, this deponent answereth and saith for his answer to this
            interogatory so far as relates to the times when I P Pendleton took the Petioner to
            Virginia with him he refers to his answer to the 3rd Interogatory on the part of the
            defendant. And to the latter part of this Interogatory he canot answer.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>To the 2nd Interogatory, this deponent cannot answer</p>
         
         
         <p>the foregoing Deposition made and subscribed by me this 3rd day of december 1838</p>
         
         
         <p>Abraham Jump</p>
         
         
         <p>State of Maryland Caroline County Set. On this 3rd day of December 1838 personally
            appeared before the subscriber one of the State of Maryland Justices of the Peace
            in and for the County aforesaid, Abraham Jump who made and subscribed the foregoing
            deposition, and who being sworn on the Holy Evangels of Almighty God, deposeth and
            saith, that the several facts stated in the foregoing deposition are true and that
            they are his answers to the several interrogatories therein stated.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>Hugh Taylor</p>
