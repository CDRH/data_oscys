         <p>District of Columbia, to wit:<br/>
The United States of America,<br/>
To the Marshal of the District of Columbia, Greeting:</p>

      </div>

      <div class="body">

         <p>You are hereby commanded to summon Bernard H Buckner and Aris Buckner if they shall be found in the County of Washington, that (all excuses set aside) them be and appear before the Circuit Court of the District of Columbia, to be held for the County of Washington, in the City of Washington, on the fourth Monday of November next, to <del>testify on the part of</del> answer the petition for freedom of Cassius Jackson, otherwise called Cassius, filed in said Court against them.</p>

         <p>Hereof fail not at your peril; and have you then and there this precept.</p>

         <p>Witness W. Cranch Esquire, Chief Judge of the said Court.</p>

         <p>Issued the 25 day of September 1833.</p>

         <p>W. Brent, Clk</p>

         <p>Dermott.</p>
