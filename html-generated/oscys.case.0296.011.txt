         <p>To the Honbl<br/>
The Judges of the Circuit Court of the District of Columbia</p> 

      </div>

      <div class="body ">
    
         <p>We the Jurors in the case of <del>the</del> Ellen Johnson &amp; children vs. Morris Adler admr of Thos Turner, respectfully represent to Your honors, that we have been in the Jury room nineteen hours</p>

         <p>We stand now just as we did when we came to the room, and there is not the slightest probability of our ever agreeing. One of our fellow Jurors has been sick all night and is quite unwell this morning. Under these circumstances we would most respectfully ask your honors to discharge us.</p>

         <p>On behalf of the Jury<br/>
A. W. Miller<br/>
Foreman</p>

         <p>Jury Room<br/>
December 14, 1849</p> 

         <p>I consent to the Jury being discharged<br/>
Jno Marbury for deft<br/>
1849<br/>
Dec 14<br/>
1/2 past 1 Ock</p>

         <p>I agree if Mr Carlisle will, and if he is absent, this will do<br/>
Jos. H. Bradley</p>

         <p>I called at Mr. Carlisle's office and that he has gone to the Country<br/>
W. H. Barnacle</p>
