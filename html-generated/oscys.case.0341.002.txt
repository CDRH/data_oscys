         <p>District of Columbia, to wit:<br/>
The United States of America,<br/>
To the Marshal of the District of Columbia, Greeting:</p>

      </div>

      <div class="body">

         <p>Whereas, at a Circuit Court of the district of Columbia, held for the county of Washington, at the city of Washington, on the Fourth Monday of December in the year of our Lord one thousand eight hundred and twenty four a certain Negro Rebecca and children, William Ann &amp; Margaret by judgment of the said Court, recovered against a certain Lloyd Pumphrey as well <del>the sum of</del> their freedom on their petition then before the same court against the said Lloyd Pumphrey on the sum of Forty one dollars and sixty eight cents, which was then and there adjudged unto the said Negro Rebecca and children for their costs and charges by them about this prosecution of the said petition laid out and expended; <del>which was adjudged by the said Court to the said       on     assent for       costs and charges by       about       suit in that behalf laid out and expended;</del> whereof the said Lloyd Pumphrey is convict as it appearer of record. Therefore you are hereby commanded to take the said Lloyd Pumphrey if he shall be found in the said county of Washington, and him safely keep so that you have his body before the Circuit Court of the district of Columbia, to be held for the county of Washington, at the city of Washington, on the First Monday of May next, to satisfy unto the said Negro Rebecca &amp; children the <del>damages</del> costs, and charges aforesaid. Hereof fail not, at your peril: and have you then and there this writ.</p>

         <p>Witness, William Cranch, Esquire, chief judge of the said Court, the 9th day of February in the year 1825.</p>

         <p>Issued the 9th day of March 1825</p>

         <p>Wm. Brent, Clk</p>

         <p>Turner<br/>
C. Cox</p>

      </div>
