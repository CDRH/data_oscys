         <p>If the jury believe from the evidence that the said George Graham came to the city of Washington in the year 1814, &amp; resided there till his death, that he remained there &amp; had children born there, that he purchased <del>real property</del> a house &amp; lot there &amp; lived in it till his death, that he was in the year 1821 or 1822, the owner of the Petitioner then &amp; for some <del>time</del> years previous, residing in Virginia, &amp; that he then ordered the said Petr to come to this city, that he did come here by reason of such order, &amp; was held here as a slave by the said George, until the time of his death, then the jury may infer that the said George imported the said slave with intent to reside &amp; is entitled to his freedom.</p>
