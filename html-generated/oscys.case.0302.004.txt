         <p>District of Columbia, to wit:<br/> 
The United States of America,<br/>
To the Marshal of the District of Columbia—Greeting:</p>

      </div>

      <div class="body">
     
         <p>We command you that you <del>take</del> Summon Thomas Ogden <del>late of Washington County, if shall be found within the County of Washington in your said District, and safely keep, so that you have bod</del> before the Circuit Court of the District of Columbia, to be held for the County aforesaid, at the City of Washington, on the 4th Monday of March next, to answer <del>unto</del> the petition of freedom of Ann Kane.</p>

         <p>Hereof fail not, at your peril, and have you then and there this writ.</p>

         <p>Witness, J. Dunlop Esquire, Chief Judge of our said Court, at the City of Washington, the 14t day of June Anno Domini one thousand eight hundred and 55</p>

         <p>Issued the 26th day of December 1855.</p>

         <p>Jno. A. Smith. clk</p>

         <p>Ratcliff.</p>
