         <p>If the Jury believe from the evidence aforesaid that the petitioner was a slave of
            Tho Turner Senr in his life time &amp; at his death came to the possession of Trueman
            Tyler his Exr, 
            <del class="who returned">who returned</del> and that the said Exr did return the petitioner as a slave of the Testators Estate
            to the orphans court of Wash: County in this district &amp; died without having made any
            
            <span class="strikethrough">[strikethrough]</span> distribution there of or of any part there of among the next of kin of said Turner
            and no voluntary distribution thereof was made among 
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span> next of kin. and after the death of the said Executor no further admr of said Estate
            was taken out or granted by the said Court, &amp; that the petitioner remained in the
            possession of Tho Turner Junr son &amp; one of five children &amp; distributees of the said
            Turner Senr as part of his fathers Estate subject to the claims of his co-distributees.
            then no act or declaration of the said Turner Junr can operate to give freedom to
            the said petitioner, 
            <del class="on">on</del> without the concurrence of his co-distributees or authorise the Jury to presume a
            deed of manumission had been ever to the petitioner &amp; without such a presumption the
            Jury must find a verdict for the defendant.
         </p>
