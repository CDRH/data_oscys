         <p>Know all men by these presents that We William A. Scott and William Yeates are held
            and firmly bound unto Jonathan Davis &amp; James Kincaid in the full &amp; just sum of Eight
            hundred Dollars current money of The United States to be paid to the said Jonathan
            &amp; James or their certain attorney executors administrators and assigns to which payment
            We bind 
            <del class="myself">myself</del> 
            <del class="my">my</del> ourselves our heirs Exrs. and admrs. by these presents sealed with our seals and
            dated this eighteenth day of May A.D. 1816.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>The condition of the above obligation is such that if the above bound William A. Scott
            do &amp; shall well &amp; truly produce before the Honorable the Judges of the Circuit Court
            of The District of Columbia for the County of Washington on the first day of the next
            Term of the said Court in Washington unless prevented by death or other inevitable
            precident Negro Morris lately sold by one E. Hale to George Miller as the agent of
            Colonel Jonathan Davis or of Davis &amp; Kincaid and in the meantime do &amp; shall support
            and maintain the said Negro Morris, and shall indemnify &amp; 
            <span class="illegible">[save?]</span> 
            <span class="illegible">[harmly?]</span>, the said Davis &amp; Kincaid their heirs executors &amp; administrators from all costs damages
            &amp; charges that may accrue by reason of the filing of a Petition for freedom by said
            Morris against Davis &amp; Kincaid and of the proceedings under the same if the said Morris
            shall not establish &amp; prove his claim to Freedom according to Law, and do &amp; shall
            will &amp; truly pay to said Davis &amp; Kincaid their heirs or assigns reasonable wages and
            hire for the said Negro Morris at the rate of Ten Dollars per month if he shall be
            well &amp; able to earn wages from this time until he shall be produced according to this
            condition the said wages to be paid only in case of the failure of the Petitioner
            in his claim to freedom. Then the

         <p>In witness whereof we hereto set our hands &amp; seals the day &amp; year aforesaid</p>
