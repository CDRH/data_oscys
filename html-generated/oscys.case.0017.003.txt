         <p>Davis v Forrest and Davis v Minifee</p>
         
         
      </div>
      
      
      <div class="body ">
         
         
         <p>At the trial of these causes Petrs offered to examine as witnesses in the cause two
            free negroes towit one Susan Davis &amp; one Rosamond Bentley; who recovered their freedom
            in the 
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span> set forth in the two records 
            <del class="to the adm">to the adm</del> and the Defts in these causes being free christian white persons, objected to the
            admission of the said free negroes as 
            <del class="evidence">evidence</del> witnesses; but the court overruled the objection and admitted them to be sworn &amp;
            examined as witnesses on behalf of the Petitioners. To which the Defts except, and
            this their bill of exceptions is sealed &amp; recorded.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>N. Fitzhugh (Seal)
            <br></br>
            B Thruston (Seal)
         </p>
         
         
         <p>Note; This Decision results from this point having been settled 
            <del class="in this way formerly;">in this way formerly;</del> by former precedents; had it been 
            <em>Res integra</em> the Opinion of the Subscriber would have been the other way.
            <br></br>
            B Thruston
