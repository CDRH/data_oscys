         <p>Sally Baker &amp; children<br/>
vs<br/>
Charles Hay</p>

         <p>Petition of Freedom<br/>
Agreement</p>

      </div>

      <div class="body ">

         <p>It is agreed by and between the parties to this case that the petition shall be dismissed. Mr. George Hay agreeing to pay the legal taxed costs in the case and the jail fees due for the detention of Sally, last night.</p>

         <p>The petitioners agree to surrender themselves to Judge Hay and return with him peaceably and quietly to his home in Virginia. he on his part promises to forgive what is past and if Sally behaves herself well to treat her with kindness.</p>

         <p>The petitioner Sally having had her right and her situation <del>as</del> fully explained and stated by her counsel and friends freely voluntarily and without any fear <span class="gap illegible">[illegible]</span> or compulsion in open court waives her claim for freedom under the foregoing conditions.</p>

         <p>Richard S. Coxe<br/>
Atty for defdt and George Hay.</p>

         <p>June 8th 1826</p>

         <p>Wm Bussard for petitioner</p>
