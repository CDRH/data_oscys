         <p>We of the Jury find that negro Will Thornton the Petitioner for freedom, was a Virginia
            born slave. And was held by Mrs. Graham the mother of the late George Graham for her
            life and after her death he was the property of the said George Graham. That the mother
            of the said George Graham resided in Virga. and the said negroe William resided 
            <del class="with">with</del> there also 
            <del class="here">here</del>, until her death which happened in the year 1819. That at the time of the death of
            Mrs. Graham, negro William was hired out to Mr. Jno. Stith of Virginia, and the said
            George Graham 
            <del class="sent for">sent for</del> then a resident of the City of Washington sent for the said slave (with intent to
            carry him from Washington with his other slaves to the State of Kentucky). That the
            petitioner did not come so as to go with the other slaves, and the said George Graham
            left Washington with his other slaves without taking with him the said negro William.
            That after the said Geo. Grahams departure as aforesaid for Kentucky, and during his
            absence from Washington, the said negroe William came to Washington that is to say
            on or about the 
            <del class="year">year</del> last of April 1819 
            <del class="1820">1820</del> and has remained here ever since. That the said George Graham returned from Kentucky
