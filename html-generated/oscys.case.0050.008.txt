         <h4>William Jordan vs Lemuel Sawyer</h4>
         
         
      </div>
      
      
      <div class="body ">
         
         
         <p>The Jury in this case do find from the evidence that the Petitioner was brought into
            this County sometime early in January last by one Vale. Peyton, which said Peyton
            was not then, before or since, a resident of this County or District, but 
            <del class="came with">came with</del> brought the said 
            <del class="negro">negro</del> petitioner, from Wheeling in Virginia into this County, and that sometime in March
            last he sold the said Petitioner to the Defendant in the City of Washington, &amp; that
            it was the intent both of the Seller &amp; Purchaser (and so agreed at the time of the
            
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span>) that the said Petitioner should be sent 
            <del class="by the Defendant">by the Defendant</del> by the Defendant forthwith the Norfolk in Virginia 
            <del class="but that no such intent was expressed in the Bill of Sale made by said Peyton">but that no such intent was expressed in the Bill of Sale made by said Peyton</del> and that he was forth with upon such sale put on board the steamboat &amp; sent to Norfolk
            
            <del class="by the Deft">by the Deft</del> as the property of the Deft. and that upon his being 
            <del class="delivered">delivered</del> put in the said steamboat, the purchase money was paid by the Deft. That afterwards
            the said Petr returned in the said steamboat from Norfolk, &amp; filed his petition.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>And we do further find that the said Val: Peyton was not a person coming into this
            
            <del class="said">said</del> County to reside therein, but as a Sojourner or Traveller, and that he did not carry
            the said 
            
            
            <span class="hr"> </span>
            <span class="pageimage">
               <a href="http://jetson.unl.edu/oscys_figures/large/oscys.case.0050.008.002.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://jetson.unl.edu/oscys_figures/large/oscys.case.0050.008.002.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;">
                  <img src="http://jetson.unl.edu/oscys_figures/thumbnails/oscys.case.0050.008.002.jpg" class="display"></img>
               </a>
            </span>
            
            said Petitioner with him when he went away without selling him, or attempt to carry
            him away without selling him. Also that 
            <del class="said Peyton">said Peyton</del> there is no evidence from which the Jury can find that the said Peyton held lands
            in another state so as to authorize him to import said Petitioner
         </p>
         
         
         <p>brought the said Petitioner into this County under any of the circumstances set forth
            in the provisos contained in the 
            <del class="5, 6">5, 6</del> 7th, 8th, 9th, 10 &amp; 11th sections of the act of 1795 chap: 67.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>And if the Court shall be of opinion that, under the facts and circumstance set forth
            as aforesaid, 
            <del class="that">that</del> the said Petitioner is entitled to his freedom, then we find for the Petitioner,
            But if the Court shall be of opinion that under the facts &amp; circumstances aforesaid
            the Petitioner is not entitled to his freedom, then we find for the Deft.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>John B Forrest
            <br></br>
            Foreman
         </p>

            this case.
         </p>
