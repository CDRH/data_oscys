         <p>Petition for freedom.</p>

      <div class="body">

         <p>1st. Will of Parson Brown devises negro girl called Molly to his daughter Elizabeth Brown until she shall arrive at the age of 31 &amp; then to be free &amp; "all the offspring of said Molly to be free as they arrive at the age of 31." (Molly is grandmother of Petr.)</p>

         <p>2d. In 1813 Mrs Francis Alexander purchased Milly &amp; her children from Miss E. Brown (see Mrs Swann's deposition). Miss E. Brown came to live in Alexandria County between 1808 &amp; 1810, but did not bring Milly untill about 1813. She likewise lived in Georgetown D.C. &amp; Baltimore (see said deposition).</p>

         <p>3d Will of Mrs F. Alexander giving Rachael to her son Lee Masi Alexander, to this will is attached a schedule of slaves to be free at 31, signed by the Admr. R. B. Alexander among whom is Rachael the Petr. Will proved in 1823.</p>

         <p>4th Will of E. Brown in which she recites having sold to her sister Mrs F. Alexander Milly &amp; her children that her children are to serve until 31 years of age &amp; then makes provision for the purchase by her Executor of the remainder of their term of service &amp; for their <span class="gap illegible">[illegible]</span> emancipation: will dated 1825.</p>
