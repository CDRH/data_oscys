<p>Interrogatories to be put to Charles A. Stokes in a suit of
Petitioner for freedom by John Battaile a negro against
Thomas Miller Junr. in the Circuit Court of the
District of Columbia for the County of
Washington; the said Stokes being about to go
above 100 miles from the place where said suit is to be heard.</p>

<p>Interrogatory 1. Do you or do you not know any thing concerning the sale of the
petitioner by one James R. Miller if you state the time of
said sale— the consideration, and the place where the said sale was made
and finally consummated?</p>

<p>Interrogatory 2. Do you or do you not know that at the time of said sale there
was a distinct understanding between said J.R. Miller that
the said slave was to be carried out of the District of
Columbia to the State of Georgia?</p>

<p>Interrogatory 3. Do you or do you not know that after the said sale and purchase
the petitioner broke jail in the town of Alexandria and
was a runaway for a considerable length of time thereafter?</p>

<p>Interrogatory 4th Do you or do you not know that the said J.R.
Miller sold said slave with great reluctance and from <span class="unclear illegible">[these?]</span> <span class="gap illegible">[illegible]</span> and that he did it
without the knowledge of his father and family and was exceedingly anxious to
conceal from his parents the fact that he had sold him?</p>

<p>Interrogatory 5th Do you or do you not know that Thomas
Miller the defendant in said suit is the father of the said

<span class="hr"> </span><span class="pageimage"><a href="http://cdrhsites.unl.edu/figures/oscys/large/oscys.case.0083.006.002.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://cdrhsites.unl.edu/figures/oscys/large/oscys.case.0083.006.002.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;"><img src="http://cdrhsites.unl.edu/figures/oscys/thumbnails/oscys.case.0083.006.002.jpg" class="display"/></a></span>

J.R. Miller and had raised the petitioner who had been given
when a child to said J.R. Miller when he was a child by
some of his relations in the State of
Virginia? If yea state whether the defendant did not agree to refund
to you the purchase money which you had paid to his said son for the purpose of
cancelling and annulling said sale so that the petitioner might return to the
family and remain as he had been before the said sale.</p>

<p>Interrogatory 6. Do you or do you not know? and if yea state if the petitioner
was not a runaway at the time when the defendant agreed to refund as aforesaid;
also what appeared to be the motive of the defendant for doing so; and if you
did or did not commit to that arrangement</p>

<p>Interrogatory 7th. Have you or have you not any interest in the event of the said suit.</p>

<p>Interrogatory 8th Did you or did you not deliver to the defendant the receipt and
bill of sale which said J.R. Miller had given you at the
time of the said sale/</p>

<p>Interrogatory 9th<br/>
Do you or do you not know whether the negro the Petitioner was in jail in
Alexandria? if yea was he there at the time of the
agreement for the sale between yourself &amp; J.R. Miller</p>
