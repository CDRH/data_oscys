<p>District of Columbia, to wit:<br></br>
The United States of America,<br></br>
To the Marshal of the District of Columbia, Greeting:</p>

</div>

<div class="body ">
     
<p>You are hereby commanded to summon Jane W. Stevenson if she shall be found in the County of Washington, that she be and appear in the Circuit Court of the District of Columbia, <del class="at rules">at rules</del> to be held for the county of Washington, at the City of Washington, on the <del class="first">first</del> 3d Monday of October next, to answer <del class="to bill of complaint of">to bill of complaint of</del> the Petition for Freedom of Benjamin Qualls filed in Court here against her <del class="and others">and others</del>.</p>

<p>Hereof fail not at your peril; and have you then and there this precept.</p>

<p>Witness the Hon. W. Cranch, Chief Judge of the said Court.</p>

<p>Issued the 8th day of April 1845.</p>

<p>Wm Brent, Cl</p>

<p>C. S. Wallach</p>

<p>Memorandum: The defendant to enter appearance in the suit, on or before the day on which the above subpoena is returnable, otherwise the bill may be taken pro confesso.</p>
