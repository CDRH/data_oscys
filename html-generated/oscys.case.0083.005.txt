<p>Cross Interrogatories by Petitioner</p>

</div>

<div class="body ">

<p>1. Was Mr McCandly's who paid the money your agent for
purchasing negroes?</p>

<p>2. Did he or you advertize for negroes, on this side of the river?</p>

<p>3. Was McCandly's at Alexa
when the agreement was made by you &amp; Mr.
Miller?</p>

<p>4. Where was he?</p>

<p>5. Where does he reside?</p>

<p>6. How long after the agreement was it, before the money was paid by
McCandly's?</p>

<p>7. Was the money paid after you came up?</p>

<p>8. Did Miller have any conversation with
McCandly's about the sale, before he saw you at
Alexa?</p>

<p>9. Did he say <del>asu</del> how he came to call on you about it?</p>

<p>10. Where was the Petitioner when the agreement was made?</p>

<p>11. When was he brought to Alexa?</p>

<p>12. What receipt was it wh: you looked at just <span class="unclear illegible">[soon?]</span> &amp; by which you ascertained the time
of the sale?</p>

<p>13. How do you know the facts in the Defendents 4th Interrogatory</p>

<p>14th. How do you know Mr Thos Miller's motive for
repurchasing the Petitioner from you?</p>

<p>15. Who put the Petitioner in jail?</p>

<p>16. Do you know where James R. Miller and Thomas
Miller resided at the time of the sale?</p>

<p>17. Do you know anything of the Petitioner having been imported into this
District from Virginia?</p>

<p>18th. Why then, did you object to receive him on this side the river?</p>

<p>19. How long after the sale by J. R. Miller did Mr
Thos Miller apply to you, to purchase the Petitioner?</p>

<p>20. To what distance are you going from the City of
Washington. When do you go &amp; how long will you be absent.</p>

<p>B.L. Lear for Petitioner.</p>
