To the Marshal of the District of Columbia, Greeting:</p>

<div class="body ">

<p>We command you that you <del>take</del> Sally Cross late of Washington County, if she shall be found within the County of Washington in your said District, <del>and safely</del> keep, so that <del>you</del> she <del>have</del> appear <del>bod</del> before the Circuit Court of the District of Columbia, to be held for the County aforesaid, at the City of Washington, on the fourth Monday of March next, to answer unto the Petition of Sally Cross for her freedom.</p>

<p>Hereof fail not, at your peril, and have you then and there this writ.</p>

<p>Witness Wm Cranch Esquire, Chief Judge of our said Court, at the City of Washington, the 4th day of Jany Anno Domini one thousand eight hundred and forty five.</p>

<p>Issued the 25th day of Jany 1845.</p>

<p>Wm Brent Clk</p>
