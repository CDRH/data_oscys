         <p>Saint Mary's County Court March Term 1797.</p>

         <p>William Thomas<br/>
v<br/>
Edmund Plowdon Exor of Ignatius Wheeler</p>

      </div>

      <div class="body ">

         <p>On the fifteenth day of April in the year of our Lord <del>Eig</del> seventeen hundred &amp; ninety five; the petitioner by John Allen Thomas my attorney filed in Court have the following petition towit.</p>

         <p>To the Honorable the Judges of Saint Marys County Court. The petition of William Thomas, humbly sheweth; that your petitioner is lineally descended in the female line from a free white woman, and is entitled to freedom but is unjustly held in slavery by a certain Ignatius Wheeler of Saint Marys County, he therefore prays your worships to grant him relief in the premises and <del>du</del> as in duty bound will pray &amp;c</p>

         <p>John A Thomas for petitioner</p>

         <p>On the thirteenth day of March in the year one thousand seven hundred and ninety seven, the Defendant by Walter Dorssey his attorney filed in Court here the following plea towit</p>     

         <p>And the said Edmund by Walter Dorssey his attorney comes and saith that the said William Thomas, is not lineally descended in the female line from a free white woman, and of this he puts himself upon the Country, and the said William doth the like andsoforth</p>

         <p>Walter Dorsey for Deft</p>

         <p>Jury Sworn. Verdict for the Defendant</p>

         <p>Judgment on the Verdict</p>

         <p>True Extract, Jo. Harris Clk</p>
