         <p>The Petition of, Letty Brown, for 
            <del class="a">a</del> her self &amp; infant child Robert Brown respectfully sheweth.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>That about the year 1817 or 1818, 
            <del class="John P. Lowe">John P. Lowe</del> she being about to be sold by her mistress, Mary Greenfield, and hoping to make an
            arrangement by wh: she might obtain her freedom, applied to one John H Lowe of the
            City of Washington (who now claims her &amp; her child as slaves) to assist her in raising
            money to obtain her freedom; that it was agreed between them that your petitioners
            should advance forty dollars wh: she then had of her own money &amp; the said Lowe should
            advance or become 
            <del class="resp">resp</del> responsible for the balance of the purchase money (viz 210 dollars) and that the
            said Lowe should hold her 
            <span class="illegible">[nominally?]</span> as a slave mostly to secure to him the repayment of his advance, as fast as she could
            raise the money. That she has raised &amp; paid to the said Lowe 235 dollars of said money
            (including the 40 advanced by her) &amp; is ready to pay him the balance due upon his
            consenting to carry into effect his aforesaid agreement with her.
         </p>  
         
         
         <span class="hr"> </span>
         <span class="pageimage">
            <a href="http://earlywashingtondc.org/files/figures/large/p002.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://earlywashingtondc.org/files/figures/large/p002.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;">
               <img src="http://earlywashingtondc.org/files/figures/thumbnails/p002.jpg" class="display"></img>
            </a>
         </span>
         
         
         <p>But that so far from this, he now claims to consider the advance or responsibility
            aforesaid as a bona fide purchase of your petitioner as a slave for life &amp; actually
            now claims her &amp; her infant child aforesaid, as slaves; Whereas your Petitioner concieves
            that she is, &amp; has been entitled to her freedom ever since the agreement aforesaid
            between her &amp; the said Lowe upon her completing the repayment of the recovery advanced
            by him. Your Petitioner therefore prays your Honors to decree her freedom &amp; that of
            her aforesaid Child &amp; as in duty bound will ever pray &amp; c
         </p>
         
         
         <p>B.L. Lear for Petr</p>
         
         
         <p>Thomas S. Bingey near the glass house is Letty's witness.</p> 
         
         
         <p>Wm Brent,
            <br></br> 
            Please file this Petition &amp; issue Subpoena for Deft.
            <br></br>
            B.L. Lear
            <br></br>
            16 July 1825.
         </p>
