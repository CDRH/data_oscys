         <p>District of Columbia, to wit:</p>
         
         
         <p>The United States of America,</p>
         
         
         <p>To the Marshal of the District of Columbia, greeting:</p>
         
         
      </div>
      
      
      <div class="body ">
         
         
         <p>You are hereby commanded to summon Benjamin King if he shall be found in the county
            of Washington, that (all excuses set aside) he be and appear before the Circuit
            Court of the District of Columbia, to be held for the
            county of Washington, in
            the city of Washington, on the first Monday of
            October next, to 
            <del class="testify on the part of">testify on the part of</del>
            answer the
            petition for freedom of Jane Sims in the said court
            exhibited against him
         </p>
         
         
         <p>Hereof fail not at your peril; and have you then and there this precept.</p> 
         
         
         <p>Witness W. Cranch Esquire, chief Judge of the said Court.</p>
         
         
         <p>Issued the 14 day of May 1823</p>
         
         
         <p>Wm Brent Clk</p>
         
         
         <p>(K &amp; D)</p>
