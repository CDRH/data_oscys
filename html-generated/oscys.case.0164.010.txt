<p>District of Columbia, to wit:<br></br> 
The United States of America,<br></br>
To the Marshal of the District of Columbia, Greeting:</p>

</div>

<div class="body ">
     
<p>We command you that you <del class="take">take</del> sum Jane Monroe late of Washington County, if she shall be found within the County of Washington in your said District, and her safely keep, so that you have her body before the Circuit Court of the District of Columbia, to be held for the County aforesaid, at the City of Washington, on the 3d Monday of October next, to answer unto Jane Woodward her petition for freedom &amp;c.</p>
    
<p>Hereof fail not, at your peril, and have you then and there this writ.</p>
    
<p>Witness W Cranch Esquire, Chief Judge of our said Court and the City of Washington, the 15th day of May Anno Domini one thousand eight hundred and 51.</p>
    
<p>Issued the 23d day of May 1851.</p>
    
<p>Jno. A. Smith clk</p>
    
<p>Addison</p>
