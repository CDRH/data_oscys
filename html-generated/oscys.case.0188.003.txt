            The United States of America,
            <br></br>
            To the marshal of the District of Columbia, Greeting:
         </p>

      <div class="body ">
         
         
         <p>You are hereby commanded to summon John H. Bayne if he shall be found in the county
            of Washington, that (all excuses set aside) he be and appear before the Circuit Court
            of the district of Columbia, to be held for the county of Washington, in the city
            of Washington, on the first Monday of May next to 
            <del class="testify on the part of">testify on the part of</del> answer the petition for freedom of Nelly Lemon for herself and her infant children
            Thomas, Levinia, Betsey, Dennis and Amy, filed in said court against him
         </p>
         
         
         <p>Hereof fail not at your peril; and have you then and there this Precept.</p>
         
         
         <p>Witness W Cranch Esquire, chief Judge of the said Court.</p>
         
         
         <p>Issued the 19th day of March 1830.</p>
         
         
         <p>W Brent, Clk</p>
         
         
         <p>C &amp; Smith</p>
