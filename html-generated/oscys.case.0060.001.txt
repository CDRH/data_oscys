<p>To the Honorable the Judges of the Circuit Court of the District of Columbia, for the County of Washington,</p>

<p>The Petition of John Bacchus Burvill humbly sheweth,</p>

<p>That your Petitioner was a slave belonging to William Grayson of           County in Virginia at the time of his Death. That the Executor of said Grayson's Estate is one William Melvin. that he left a widow who has since intermarried with one       Beale, and two sons who he believes are still living, to wit. John Grayson &amp; Peter Grayson. Your Petitioner was brought or sent from said           County in Virginia about the       in the year Eighteen hundred and     into the County of Washington &amp; District of Columbia, to reside therein, contrary to the provisions of the act of Assembly of Maryland, then &amp; now of force in the said County of Washington, whereby (as he is advised) he is entitled to his freedom, &amp; prays your honors to allow him a trial therefor &amp; his discharge from slavery if he shall be found Entitled to it.</p>

<p>B L Lear for Petr</p>
