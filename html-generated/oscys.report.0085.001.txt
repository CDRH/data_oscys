         <p>Negro Humphries v. Tench.</p>

      </div>

      <div class="syllabus"> 

         <p>Depositions taken in another suit for freedom, by one of the same family, cannot be read in evidence as hearsay respecting the condition of their common ancestor.</p>

      </div>

      <div class="body">

         <p>Petition for freedom. The defendant offered to read the depositions in a record of Charles County Court in Maryland, in a suit for freedom, by one of the same family of negroes, as hearsay in relation to the common ancestor of that family.</p>

         <p>Mr. Key and Mr. Caldwell, for the petitioner, objected and cited <span class="bibl treatise">1 Phillips on Ev. 190</span>.</p>

         <p>Mr. Smith and Mr. Swann, <em class="term">contrà</em>, cited <span class="bibl treatise">1 Phil. on Ev. 174; Wheat. Dig. 153, § 16</span>.</p>

         <p>The Court (<em class="term">nem. con.</em>) said that the depositions could not be read in evidence, to prove the condition of the ancestor, (Airy.)</p>

         <p>Verdict for the petitioner.</p>

      </div>
