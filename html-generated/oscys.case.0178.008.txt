<p>Know all men by these presents that I Jacob Hoffman of the Town of Alexandria in the district of Columbia did about the year 1813, Sell for the term of Ten years the bearer hereof Clary, to Samuel Harper then of this place, the said Clary with her Issue to be free at the expiration of the said ten years. The said Harper sold her for the remainder of her time to Ruben Dye then of Alexandria &amp; he to Jonathan C May, and he to Thomas Patton and he to a Mr Henderson <span class="torn page">[torn page]</span> City and he to John C. Wilson and <span class="torn page">[torn page]</span> residue of her time to a Mr. Gold<span class="torn page">[torn page]</span> at the expiration of the said ten years the parties gave her liberty but referred her to the subscriber for her freedom papers. I do hereby manumit set free and fully discharge from my service the said Clary to Enjoy her liberty together with her three children, William a son now about nine years old, Elizabeth a daughter about three years old and a second daughter named <span class="illegible">[Martham?]</span> now about seven months old. The said Children to be subject to <span class="torn page">[torn page]</span> 

<span class="hr"> </span><span class="pageimage"><a href="http://jetson.unl.edu/oscys_figures/large/oscys.case.0178.008.002.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://jetson.unl.edu/oscys_figures/large/oscys.case.0178.008.002.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;"><img src="http://jetson.unl.edu/oscys_figures/thumbnails/oscys.case.0178.008.002.jpg" class="display"></img></a></span>

<p>Given under my hand and seal this 27th May 1829</p>

<p>Jacob Hoffman (seal)</p>
