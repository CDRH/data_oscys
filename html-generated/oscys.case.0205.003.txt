<p>That if the Jury shall believe from the evidence that by the agreement under which Mrs. Wallingford took possession of the petitioners, she was only to have the possession of them during her life upon the expiriation of which they were to belong to Mr Wallingford the deft in this cause, then the deed of manumission offered in evidence by Petitioners cannot operate against the deft in this cause if the Jury shall also find that Mrs Wallingford is dead.</p>

That then should the Jury believe from the said evidence. That she did not comply with this condition, and that she did <span class="illegible">[profess?]</span> against him a subsequent claim for <span class="illegible">[alimony?]</span> that then the said agreement cannot be enforced against the defendant nor can he be deprived of any of his rights by virtue of said agreement</p>
