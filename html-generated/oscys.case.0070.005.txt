<div class="caption ">

<p>The separate plea &amp; disclaimer of George Milburn to the petition of negro William</p>

</div>

<div class="body ">

<p>The said George Milburn in proper person, (protesting that he never was owner or proprietor, nor, in any manner, possessed of the said petitioner; that he never had or claimed any interest whatever in the petitioner, but under a conditional contract with one John Coburn in the said petition named, for the purchase of the petitioner; the execution of which contract depended on a contingency which has never happened; that <del>within</del> the said John Coburn himself, never had (aas this disclaimant is now well informed &amp; believes) any right or little whatever to the petitioner, he the petitioner bond or free) comes &amp; now here disclaims in open court all &amp; all manner of right, title, property, claim &amp; demand whatsoever, in or upon the said petitioner, as a slave or otherwise; and in like manner, disclaims &amp; renounces all interest in the question or issue whether the said petitioner be bond or free; wherefore he prays to be hence dismissed, without further or other plea or answer to the said petition, &amp;c. &amp;c.</p>

<p>Geo Milburn</p>

<p>Signed &amp; sworn to in open Court by Geo. Milburn</p>

<p>June 1st 1826. Test Wm Brent Clk</p>

</div>
