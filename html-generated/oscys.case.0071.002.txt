         <p>Martha Straas, widow, makes oath that the negro man William now in gaol, on account
            of his petition for freedom against       Milburn, was the property of the late Dr.
            W. W. Southall of Nelson County in Virginia, son of this deponent, who died intestate,
            leaving this deponent, 
            <del class="his attorney to soon">his attorney to soon</del>hi two brothers &amp; three sisters, entitled, as next of kin to the distribution of the
            surplus of his personal estate: that Christopher J. Estes duly administered on the
            estate of said intestate; and had the said negro regularly inventoried &amp; appraised
            as a part of the estate of the intestate; this deponent doth not yet certainly know
            how the estate may finally wind up; or what surplus there
            
            
            <span class="hr"> </span>
            <span class="pageimage">
               <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/data_images/projects/example/figures/large/p002.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://rosie.unl.edu/data_images/projects/example/figures/large/p002.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;">
                  <img src="http://rosie.unl.edu/data_images/projects/example/figures/thumbnails/p002.jpg" class="display"></img>
               </a>
            </span>
            
            may eventually be, for distribution among the next of kin: no distribution has ever
            been made of the same; but the said admr permitted this Deponent to take possession,
            as one of the eventual distributees, of said negro, and have the benefit of his service
            till the first settlement &amp; distribution of the estate unless sooner called for by
            the admr and being so in possession of the said negro, she brought him with her on
            her removal from Virginia to this city some time about the 
            <del class="20th">20th</del> 30th November 1824.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>that the said negro was purchased as a slave for life by the said intestate of a Mr.
            Cabell in Virginia and was so held by him the intestate till his death; and was always
            reputed &amp;d held as a slave for life; and as such was brought by her to
            
            
            <span class="hr"> </span>
            <span class="pageimage">
               <a href="http://rosie.unl.edu/data_images/projects/example/figures/large/p003.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://rosie.unl.edu/data_images/projects/example/figures/large/p003.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;">
                  <img src="http://rosie.unl.edu/data_images/projects/example/figures/thumbnails/p003.jpg" class="display"></img>
               </a>
            </span>
            
            this city, and ever since so held by her, subject to the call of the said admor, and
            this deponent further makes oath that her two sons Doctor Philip J Southall &amp; Valentine
            W. Southall have given up to this Deponent all their undivided interest in said negro.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>M. Stras.</p>
         
         
         <p>Sworn and subscribed to June 21st 1826
            <br></br>
            before James Ord J Peace
         </p>
         
         
         <p>And at the same time Emily Straas, daughter of the said Martha, but no wise interested
            in the said negro, makes oath that she is well acquainted with &amp; knows the facts &amp;
            circumstances stated in the said affidavit, relative to the purchase &amp; ownership of
            the said negro by her 
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span> brother the late Dr. W. W. Southall till his death, and that the said negro was always
            so held &amp; owned as a slave and without dispute, and not her mother had since had possession
            of him by permission of the admr Mr. Estes as stated by her mother in her said affidavit.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>Emily C. Stras.</p>
         
         
         <p>Sworn and subscribed to June 21, 1826 before James Ord J Peace</p>
