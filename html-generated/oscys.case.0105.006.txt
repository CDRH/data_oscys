         <p>If the Jury believe from the evidence that the petitioner was a slave in Maryland,
            &amp; was purchased  there &amp; brought to the city of Washington &amp; while here was greatly
            injured, so that she could not be removed from the room in which she was confined,
            &amp; in this situation the Defendant purchased the petitioner from her Master, in consideration
            of five dollars &amp; the board &amp; maintenance of the petitioner during her illness, then
            the petitioner is not entitled to her freedom, altho the Defendant may have permitted
            the petitioner to live
