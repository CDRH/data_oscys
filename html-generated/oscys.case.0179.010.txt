         <p>We the Jury empanelled in the above case doe specially find the following facts from the evidence, viz;</p>

         <p>That <del>before the month</del> in the month of 1789 a certain Richard Brown of Maryland died possessed of &amp; owning <span class="gap strikethrough">[strikethrough]</span> the maternal Grandmother of the Petitioner having previously executed in said state his last will &amp; testament (here insert it) &amp; that the maternal Grandmother of the Petitioner is the slave mentioned in said will by the name of Molly</p>

         <p>That from the year 1808 or 1809 until April 1813 Milly (the mother of the Petitioner) was hired out in Georgetown in the District of Columbia, by Elizabeth Brown who is the same person mentioned in the will of said Richard Brown by that name &amp; that during the whole of said period the said Milly was the property of said Elizabeth Brown</p>

         <p>That some time previous to 1794 the said Elizabeth Brown resided in said Georgetown (then in Maryland) &amp; from <del>that</del> the year 1794 she resided in Baltimore Maryland, until about the year 1808, or

         <p>That in April 1813 the said Elizabeth Brown removed the mother of the Petitioner from the <del>said</del> county of Washington in said District to the County of Alexandria in said District, with her two daughters Rachel &amp; Anne, &amp; the said Elizabeth Brown then sold the said mother of the Petitioner &amp; her two daughters to Mrs Frances Alexander as slaves for             after which sale &amp; delivery of said slaves to said Frances Alexander &amp; within a year after the importation of said slaves the Petitioner was born while his said mother was possessed by the said Frances Alexander under said contract with Miss Brown</p>
