         <p>Know all men by these presents, That we,</p>
         
         
         <p>Matilda Derrick for herself &amp; her children Lucy Matilda &amp; Louisa infants &amp;c for a
            petition for freedom in the full and just sum of two hundred dollars to be paid to
            the said Matilda Derrick certain attorney, executors, administrators or assigns: to
            which payment well and truly to be made, we bind ourselves, our heirs, executors and
            administrators, jointly and severally by these presents. Sealed with our seals, and
            dated this third day of December in the year of our Lord one thousand eight hundred
            and twenty three.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>Whereas lately at a circuit court of the United States for the district of Columbia
            in the County of Washington 
            <del class="term of said court last past">term of said court last past</del>, in a suit depending in said circuit court, wherein the said Matilda Derrick for
            herself &amp; her children Lucy, Matilda &amp; Louisa infants &amp;c Petitioners on a petition
            for freedom and the said George Mason &amp; Alexander Moore were defendants Judgment was
            rendered against the said George Mason &amp; Alexander Moore and the said George Mason
            &amp; Alexander Moore having obtained a writ of error, and filed a copy thereof in the
            clerk's office of the said circuit court, to reverse the judgment in the aforesaid
            suit, and a citation, directed to the said Matilda Derrick &amp; her children Lucy Matilda
            &amp; Louisa citing and admonishing him th
            <del class="i">i</del>em to be and appear at a Supreme Court of the United States, to be holden at Washington,
            on the first Monday of February next.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>Now the Condition of the above Obligation is Such, That if the said George Mason &amp;
            Alexander Moore shall prosecute their writ to effect, and answer all damages and costs,
            if they fail to make their plea good, then the above obligation to be void, else to
            remain in full force and virtue.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>G Mason {seal.}</p>
         
         
         <p>          {seal.}</p>
         
         
         <p>B. L. Lear {seal.}</p>
         
         
         <p>Sealed and delivered in presence of E B Caldwell</p>
         
         
         <p>appeared
            <br></br>
            J S Morsell
            <br></br>
            3 Dec 1823
         </p>
