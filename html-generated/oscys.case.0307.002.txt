<p>District of Columbia, to wit:<br></br> 
The United States of America,<br></br>
To the Marshal of the District of Columbia—Greeting:</p>

</div>

<div class="body ">

<p>We command you that you <del class="take">take</del> Summon Benjamin Davis late of Washington County, if he shall be found within the County of Washington in your said District, <del class="and safely keep, so that you have">and safely keep, so that you have</del> to appear <del class="bod">bod</del> before the Circuit Court of the District of Columbia, to be held for the County aforesaid, at the City of Washington, on the third Monday of March next, to answer <del class="unto">unto</del> the petition of freedom of Mary Jackson and her two children James &amp; William filed in court here against him</p>

<p>Hereof fail not, at your peril, and have you then and there this writ.</p>

<p>Witness, Hon James Dunlop <del class="Esquire">Esquire</del>, Chief Judge of our said Court, at the City of Washington, the 22d day of May Anno Domini one thousand eight hundred and 56.</p>

<p>Issued the 23d day of May 1856.</p>     

<p>Jno. A. Smith clk</p>

<p>(Bradley &amp; Bradley)</p>
