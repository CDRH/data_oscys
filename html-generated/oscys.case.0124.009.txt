         <p>In the case of David Keene's negroes now confined in Washington &amp; suing for their
            freedom I am requested to furnish a note on behalf of David Keene which may be used
            if necessary by counsel there who may each be so familiar with the Virginian Law.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>Negroes are emancipated in Virginia under the Laws which will be found 
            <div class="bibl citation">1 Vol. Revised Code 
               <span class="illegible">[pa?]</span> 433 sec. 53. 
            </div>
         </p>
         
         
         <p>It proscribes that any person by last will &amp; testament or any other instrument in
            writing under land  &amp; seal attested by two witnesses &amp; proved in the County or Corporation Court as acknowledged
            by the party in the Court of the County where he resides may emancipate his slaves.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>In order to do so it 
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span> be in the 
            <span class="illegible">[manner?]</span> prescribed by will properly proved &amp; 
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span> as writing proved &amp;c as acknowledged in the Court &amp;c
         </p>
         
         
         <p>No other mode than that prescribed will be effectual. 
            <span class="illegible">[For?]</span> it is decided that a deed recorded in a district Court (
            <span class="illegible">[tho?]</span> of higher 
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span>) is inadmissible evidence in a suit for Freedom see 
            <div class="bibl case">Graves &amp;c. v 
               <span class="illegible">[Niamis?]</span> 6 Mumford 191. 201 
            </div> 
            <div class="bibl code">Acts of May 1782. c 21 § 1. 1803. 1814 c 103 § 6 Rhode c 111 § 53 vol. p 433 </div>
         </p>
         
         
         <p>That a deed executed in Ohio but having reference to Virginia rule be void unless
            it conferred to the laws of 
            <div class="bibl case">Virginia Lewis v Fullerton 1 Randolph 15. 21 </div>.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>That an instrument of Emancipation is ineffectual
            
            
            <span class="hr"> </span>
            <span class="pageimage">
               <a href="http://earlywashingtondc.org/files/figures/large/p002.jpg" rel="prettyPhoto[pp_gal]" title="&lt;a href=&#34;http://earlywashingtondc.org/files/figures/large/p002.jpg&#34; target=&#34;_blank&#34; &gt;open image in new window&lt;/a&gt;">
                  <img src="http://earlywashingtondc.org/files/figures/thumbnails/p002.jpg" class="display"></img>
               </a>
            </span>
            
            to confer Freedom till full probate thereof be made &amp; takes effect only from the date
            of complete probate. See 
            <div class="bibl case">Thrift v Hannah &amp;c 2 Leigh 300 </div>. See the opinion of Judges in 
            <div class="bibl case">Mann v Givens &amp;c 7 Leigh 707 </div>.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>It seems they cannot be emancipated by a 
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span> will see opinion of 
            <div class="bibl case">Tucker &amp; Brooke </div> in 
            <div class="bibl case">Winn v Bob &amp;c 3 Leigh 140 </div>
         </p>
         
         
         <p>In this case the will is acknowledged before one witness only &amp; is therefore under
            the Laws of Virginia no will. See 
            <div class="bibl code">Sessions Acts March 1835 
               <span class="illegible">[per?]</span> 43 
            </div>
         </p>
         
         
         <p>
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span> will is decided by the Court a Court of Competent Jurisdiction. And as this Case
            must 
            <span class="illegible">[turn?]</span> on the Laws of Virginia until the decision of the Court is reversed I presume it
            is conclusive.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>B W Harrison 
            <span class="illegible">[Novr.?]</span> 1843
         </p>
         
         
         <p>A County Court refuses to receive an instrument of emancipation to probate, can be
            compelled only by 
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span>:
            
            <div class="bibl case">Mann &amp; oth vs Seporis &amp; oth. 2 Leigh 762 </div>.
            Mandamus is the remedy. 
            <div class="bibl case">7 Leigh 689 </div>.
         </p>
         
         
         <p>There is no right of emancipation in Va. except as expressly given by the Statutes,
            which must be strictly 
            <span class="illegible">[illegible]</span>. 
            <div class="bibl case">Thrift vs Hanna 2 Leigh 300 </div>.
         </p>
